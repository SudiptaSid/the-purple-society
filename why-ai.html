Always Moving Forward for Children of the World

At the forefront of advancements in the field of artificial intelligence (AI)

We are inspired by the world of artificial intelligence (AI) and the exciting changes happening within it. As experts work to improve and advance AI, a new focus has emerged on the importance of data.

AI is made up of two main components: the model and the data. The models are like tools that help computers understand information, while the data is what the models analyze. In the past, most AI developers focused on perfecting their models, known as model-centric AI. This approach led to advanced machine learning systems, but it also had limitations.

Recently, there has been a shift towards a data-centric approach to AI. This means researchers are now placing greater importance on the quality of the data used in AI models. AI trailblazer Andrew Ng defines this approach as "systematically engineering the data needed to build a successful AI system." By focusing on the data, researchers are working to improve the performance of AI models and help bring us closer to finding a cure for childhood cancer.

Here are a few ways in which AI is important in our medical research:

  • Data analysis: Medical research generates a vast amount of data, including patient records, clinical trial results, and genetic information. AI algorithms can analyze this data quickly and accurately, uncovering patterns and trends that might not be immediately apparent to humans. This can help researchers identify risk factors for diseases, develop new treatments, and predict the likelihood of certain health outcomes.
  • Diagnosis and treatment: AI algorithms can analyze patient data and help doctors make more accurate diagnoses. For example, AI can analyze medical images and identify abnormalities that might not be visible to the human eye. This can help doctors identify diseases in the early stages, when they are most treatable. AI can also help doctors identify the most appropriate treatment options for individual patients based on their unique characteristics and medical history.
  • Drug development: AI can be used to analyze large amounts of data from chemical compounds and identify those with the most potential for use in new drugs. This can help speed up the drug development process, which can take years or even decades using traditional methods.
  • Clinical trials: AI can be used to identify potential candidates for clinical trials and monitor their progress. This can help researchers enroll more representative groups of patients, which can lead to more accurate results. AI can also help researchers identify trends and patterns in the data collected during clinical trials, allowing them to make more informed decisions about which treatments are most effective.

As a supporter of The Purple Society, we want to thank you for your past donations and to let you know about the important work we are doing to help children with cancer.
